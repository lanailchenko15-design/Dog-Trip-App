Brean Down Circular ⭐
NT car park, Brean Down Cove TA8 2RS · 3 miles · Moderate
5-min drive
Time1.5–2 hours
DogsON LEAD at all times on the Down (steep cliffs, nesting birds Mar–Jul, livestock). Off lead on beach.

Somerset's 'natural pier' — a limestone peninsula extending 1.5 miles into the Bristol Channel, rising 97m. Climb 200 steps (or gentler military road) to the ridge, walk to the Palmerston Fort at the tip. Return via the lower southern path past a Romano-Celtic temple and Iron Age hillfort. Views to Wales, Steep Holm, and the Somerset Levels. Wild goats, peregrine falcons and skylarks.

Refreshments: Brean Down Café (dogs inside, doggy breakfast & ice cream) or NT Cove Café (dogs outside, covered seating). Both at car park.

RouteStart at Lulworth Cove car park (BH20 5RQ) → South West Coast Path west over Hambury Tout → descend to Durdle Door → Man O'War Bay → return via Scratchy Bottom valley and inland paths back to West Lulworth.
HighlightsPanoramic Jurassic Coast views, two dog-friendly beaches, limestone arch. Bronze Age bell barrow at top of Hambury Tout. Brilliant in late May when limestone grassland flowers are out.
